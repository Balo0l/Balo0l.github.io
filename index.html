Biography

Dr. Al-Shaer is a Distinguished Research Fellow at Institute of Software Research (ISR) in the School of Computer Science and Faculty Member of CyLab at Carnegie Mellon University. Dr. Al-Shaer has more than two decades as a researcher and educator in the field of Cybersecurity. Prior to joining ISR/CMU, Prof. Al-Shaer was a Distinguished Career Professor at the Information Networking Institute (INI) at Carnegie Mellon University and was a Professor and the Founding Director of CyberDNA and NSF Cybersecurity Analytics and Automation (CCAA) centers at the University of North Carolina Charlotte from 2009â€“2020.

His research expertise includes:

  1. Formal methods for security configuration verification, synthesis and hardening of enterprise, SDN, IoT and smart grid systems.
  2. Adaptive & automated cyber defense.
  3. Cyber and cyber-physical resilience.
  4. Data-driven cybersecurity analytics.
  5. Cyber agility for attack deterrence and deception.

Dr. Al-Shaer has published over 200 refereed papers, edited 9+ books/chapters, and graduated 14 PhD students. He was designated a DoD SME in 2011, awarded IBM Faculty Award in 2012 and UNC Charlotte Faculty Research Award in 2013. He has received over $20M in funding from agencies and industry partners.

Research

Research interests include cybersecurity analytics, automation, formal verification, SDN/IoT security, and cyber defense strategies.
